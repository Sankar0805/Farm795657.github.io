Ragi Crop Information

About Ragi Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Ragi, or Finger Millet, is a hardy crop grown in tribal belts and dryland areas like Vizianagaram and Visakhapatnam. It is rich in calcium and ideal for poor soils.

Soil Requirements

Red loamy soils with good drainage; pH 5.5–7.0

Temperature

Optimal: 25–30°C; tolerant to drought and heat

Water Needs

Low; suitable for rainfed areas

Growing Period

3.5 to 4 months depending on variety

Common Ragi Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Blast Disease

Symptoms: Lesions on leaves and neck; poor grain filling

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties like GPU-28, PR-202
  • Spray decoction of garlic + chili + cow urine (weekly)
  • Apply neem oil (3%) at flowering stage
  • Crop rotation with legumes
Bacterial

Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Dry, brown spots on leaves, premature drying

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply cow dung slurry with turmeric
  • Intercrop with pigeon pea to reduce humidity
  • Spray diluted sour buttermilk (1:3 with water)
  • Remove infected debris and maintain field hygiene
Viral

Yellowing / Mosaic

Symptoms: Yellow mosaic patterns, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray neem leaf extract (1:10 dilution) weekly
  • Use cow urine + asafoetida solution as foliar spray
  • Grow trap crops like marigold to control aphids
  • Destroy infected plants immediately
