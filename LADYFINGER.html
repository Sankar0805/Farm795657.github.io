Ladyfinger Crop Information

About Ladyfinger Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Ladyfinger (Okra) is a popular vegetable crop in Andhra Pradesh, with Visakhapatnam district being suitable for its cultivation. The crop thrives in warm conditions and is grown extensively in the region.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter, pH 6.0-6.8

Temperature

24-30°C (75-86°F) ideal for growth

Water Needs

Moderate irrigation, sensitive to waterlogging

Growing Period

45-60 days from sowing to first harvest

Common Ladyfinger Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Yellow Vein Mosaic

Symptoms: Yellow network of veins, stunted growth, small deformed fruits

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties like Parbhani Kranti
  • Control whitefly vectors with neem oil spray
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
  • Practice crop rotation with non-host crops
  • Install yellow sticky traps
Fungal

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White powdery growth on leaves, yellowing and drying of leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray milk solution (1 part milk to 9 parts water)
  • Apply baking soda spray (1 tbsp/gallon water)
  • Use neem oil or sulfur dust
  • Improve air circulation between plants
  • Remove infected plant parts
Bacterial

Bacterial Blight

Symptoms: Water-soaked spots on leaves, stem cankers, fruit rot

Organic Remedies:

  • Use disease-free seeds
  • Spray copper-based fungicides
  • Practice crop rotation (3 year cycle)
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
  • Apply garlic extract solution
