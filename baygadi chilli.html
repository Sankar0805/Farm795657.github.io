Byadagi Chilli Crop Information

About Byadagi Chilli Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Byadagi chillies are known for their deep red color and mild pungency. Ideal for food coloring and flavoring, they are a significant crop in South India.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy or black soils with pH 6.0–7.0

Temperature

Optimal between 20–30°C

Water Needs

Moderate watering; avoid waterlogging

Growing Period

150–180 days depending on variety and season

Common Byadagi Chilli Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Leaves curl upward, become thick and brittle

Organic Remedies:

  • Remove infected plants immediately
  • Use yellow sticky traps for whitefly control
  • Spray neem oil (5ml/l) weekly
Disease

Fruit Rot & Anthracnose

Symptoms: Dark sunken lesions on fruits leading to decay

Organic Remedies:

  • Ensure good drainage and proper spacing
  • Use Trichoderma-based sprays
  • Apply compost teas to improve plant immunity
