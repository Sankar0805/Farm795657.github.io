Proso Millet Crop Information

About Proso Millet in Andhra Pradesh

Proso millet, also called Varagu or Panivaragu, is a short-duration millet cultivated in dry regions of Andhra Pradesh. It's highly nutritious and a good choice for low-input farming systems.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained sandy loam to loamy soil; pH 5.5–7.5

Temperature

Optimal: 20–30°C; drought-tolerant

Water Needs

Low; ideal for rainfed cultivation

Growing Period

60–90 days depending on variety

Common Proso Millet Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Downy Mildew

Symptoms: Yellowing of leaves, white patches on underside

Organic Remedies:

  • Seed treatment with Trichoderma viride (5g/kg seed)
  • Spray diluted neem oil (3%) during early growth
  • Rotate crops with legumes
  • Maintain proper plant spacing for air flow
Fungal

Blast Disease

Symptoms: Dark brown spots on leaves and nodes

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray garlic + chili + neem decoction (weekly)
  • Apply Panchagavya (3%) at booting stage
  • Avoid excess nitrogen; use composted manure
  • Remove infected crop residues after harvest
Pests

Shoot Fly

Symptoms: Deadheart formation in young plants

Organic Remedies:

  • Use light traps to attract and trap flies
  • Spray neem kernel extract (5%) in early stages
  • Early sowing to avoid peak infestation
  • Apply ash + cow urine mixture at root zone
