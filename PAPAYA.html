Papaya Crop Information

About Papaya Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Papaya is widely cultivated in coastal and semi-arid areas of Andhra Pradesh. Red Lady and Coorg Honey Dew are the preferred varieties.

Soil Requirements

Light, well-drained soil with pH 6.0–6.7

Temperature

Optimal temperature: 21–33°C, frost sensitive

Water Needs

Moderate irrigation; avoid waterlogging

Growing Period

7–11 months for fruiting, year-round cultivation possible

Common Papaya Diseases and Organic Remedies

Viral

Papaya Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Leaf curling, stunted growth, pale foliage, fruit deformities

Organic Remedies:

  • Use virus-free seedlings from certified nurseries
  • Spray neem oil (3%) to control whiteflies
  • Use yellow sticky traps for vector monitoring
  • Rogue and destroy infected plants
Fungal

Foot Rot (Phytophthora spp.)

Symptoms: Base rot, yellowing leaves, plant collapse

Organic Remedies:

  • Improve soil drainage and avoid water stagnation
  • Apply Trichoderma viride near plant base
  • Use mulch and raised beds for air circulation
  • Spray garlic-chili extract monthly
Bacterial

Bacterial Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Water-soaked lesions on leaves, necrosis, reduced yield

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray turmeric extract (5%) for antibacterial action
  • Remove and burn infected leaves
  • Apply cow urine-based bio-spray weekly
  • Ensure plant spacing to avoid moisture retention
