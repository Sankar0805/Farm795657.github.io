Pigeon Pea Crop Information

About Pigeon Pea Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Pigeon pea is a key pulse crop grown during kharif season. It’s drought-tolerant and suitable for the semi-arid regions of Andhra Pradesh.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy to sandy loam soil, pH 6.0-7.5

Temperature

18-30°C ideal for growth and flowering

Water Needs

Moderate water; drought-resistant crop

Growing Period

140-160 days depending on the variety

Common Pigeon Pea Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Fusarium Wilt

Symptoms: Wilting, yellowing leaves, brown vascular tissues

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties
  • Apply Trichoderma harzianum to soil
  • Use neem cake in field preparation
Disease

Pod Borer

Symptoms: Boreholes in pods, larval feeding on seeds

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray neem seed kernel extract
  • Install pheromone traps
  • Introduce parasitoids like Trichogramma
Disease

Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Circular dark brown spots on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply garlic-chili extract spray
  • Use compost tea as foliar spray
  • Maintain field sanitation
