Wheat Crop Information

About Wheat Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Wheat is a significant rabi crop in Andhra Pradesh, cultivated primarily in regions with suitable winter climate. Visakhapatnam farmers are adopting wheat cultivation as part of crop diversification strategies.

Soil Requirements

Loamy soil with proper drainage, pH 6.0-7.5

Temperature

10-25°C (50-77°F) during growing season

Water Needs

Requires moderate irrigation, avoid waterlogging

Growing Period

4-5 months depending on variety

Common Wheat Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Wheat Rust

Symptoms: Rust-colored spots on leaves and stems, reduced grain quality

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant wheat varieties
  • Spray neem oil solution regularly
  • Apply compost tea to boost immunity
  • Rotate crops to break disease cycle
  • Remove infected plant debris post-harvest
Disease

Loose Smut

Symptoms: Black powdery mass replacing ears, seed-borne disease

Organic Remedies:

  • Hot water treatment of seeds before sowing
  • Use certified smut-free seeds
  • Solar drying of seeds after soaking
  • Burn infected plant parts
  • Use garlic extract sprays
Disease

Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Brown lesions with yellow borders on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply copper-based biofungicides
  • Use cow urine sprays weekly
  • Improve spacing to reduce humidity
  • Intercrop with marigold to reduce pests
  • Practice timely irrigation and weeding
