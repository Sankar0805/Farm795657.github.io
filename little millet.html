Little Millet Crop Information

About Little Millet Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Little Millet is a resilient minor cereal crop known for its quick maturity and nutritional value. It’s suitable for dryland and tribal areas.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained red, loamy, or sandy soils with pH 5.5–7.5

Temperature

Optimal between 25–30°C

Water Needs

Low water requirement; drought-resistant crop

Growing Period

75–90 days depending on conditions

Common Little Millet Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Smut Disease

Symptoms: Black powdery masses replace grains in the ear head

Organic Remedies:

  • Use certified disease-free seeds
  • Hot water seed treatment at 55°C for 10 minutes
  • Field sanitation and crop rotation
Disease

Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Brown to greyish spots on leaves with concentric rings

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray diluted neem oil weekly
  • Use compost extracts to boost resistance
  • Remove infected leaves to prevent spread
