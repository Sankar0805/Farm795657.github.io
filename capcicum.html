Capsicum Crop Information

About Capsicum in Andhra Pradesh

Capsicum, also called bell pepper or sweet pepper, is a profitable vegetable crop grown under open field and greenhouse conditions. It is popular for its color, flavor, and nutritional value.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil rich in organic matter; pH 6.0–7.0

Temperature

Optimal: 20–30°C; sensitive to frost and extreme heat

Water Needs

Moderate; avoid waterlogging and irregular watering

Growing Period

90–120 days depending on variety

Common Capsicum Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Phytophthora Root and Fruit Rot

Symptoms: Wilting, root rot, water-soaked fruits

Organic Remedies:

  • Use raised beds and well-drained soil
  • Apply Trichoderma viride at planting (10g/plant)
  • Spray 2% garlic extract biweekly
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
Viral

Capcicum Mosaic Virus (CMV)

Symptoms: Leaf mottling, distortion, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Control aphid vectors with neem oil spray (5%)
  • Use yellow sticky traps to monitor aphids
  • Remove and burn infected plants
  • Intercrop with maize or sorghum to distract vectors
Bacterial

Bacterial Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Small dark spots on leaves and fruits, yellow halos

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply Bordeaux mixture (1%) during early growth
  • Practice crop rotation with non-solanaceous crops
  • Ensure adequate spacing to improve air circulation
  • Use disease-free certified seeds
