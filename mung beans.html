Mung Beans Crop Information

About Mung Beans Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Mung beans are a vital pulse crop grown in Andhra Pradesh, especially during the kharif season. Visakhapatnam district provides favorable weather conditions for high-quality mung bean farming.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil, pH 6.0-7.5

Temperature

25-35°C (77-95°F) ideal for growth and flowering

Water Needs

Moderate irrigation, particularly during flowering and pod filling

Growing Period

60-90 days, depending on variety

Common Mung Beans Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White, powdery fungal growth on leaves and stems

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply neem oil or a mixture of baking soda and water
  • Improve air circulation in the field
  • Remove infected plant parts and debris
  • Use resistant mung bean varieties
Disease

Insect Pest Damage

Symptoms: Small holes in leaves, stunted plant growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Use natural predators like ladybugs
  • Spray neem oil or garlic-based insecticide
  • Introduce insecticidal soap solutions
Disease

Root Rot

Symptoms: Yellowing and wilting of leaves, weak root system

Organic Remedies:

  • Improve drainage in the field
  • Use bio-fungicides like Trichoderma
  • Ensure soil is well-aerated
  • Rotate crops to prevent soil-borne diseases
