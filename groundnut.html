Groundnut Crop Information

About Groundnut Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Groundnut is a major oilseed crop in Andhra Pradesh, grown during kharif and rabi seasons, especially in dryland areas.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained sandy loam or loamy soils with pH 5.5–7.0

Temperature

25–35°C is ideal for optimal yield

Water Needs

Requires moderate irrigation; avoid waterlogging

Growing Period

90–120 days depending on variety

Common Groundnut Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Early Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Brown circular spots with yellow halo on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Use Trichoderma-based sprays
  • Neem oil + garlic extract spray weekly
  • Remove and burn infected leaves
Disease

Aflatoxin Contamination

Symptoms: Moldy kernels, musty smell; not visible always

Organic Remedies:

  • Ensure proper drying before storage
  • Use bio-control agent Aspergillus flavus strain AF36
  • Avoid mechanical damage during harvest
Disease

Root Knot Nematode

Symptoms: Knots/galls on roots, stunted plant growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply neem cake before sowing
  • Use marigold as a trap crop
  • Solarize soil using plastic mulch pre-planting
