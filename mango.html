Mango Crop Information

About Mango Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Mango is the king of fruits and widely grown in Chittoor, Krishna, and Vizianagaram districts. Varieties like Banganapalli, Suvarnarekha, and Totapuri are popular.

Soil Requirements

Deep, well-drained loamy soil with pH 5.5–7.5

Temperature

Optimal: 24–30°C; avoid frost-prone zones

Water Needs

Moderate; once in 2–3 weeks during dry periods

Growing Period

Fruit production starts in 3–4 years; mature yield by 8–10 years

Common Mango Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Anthracnose

Symptoms: Black lesions on leaves, flowers, and fruit leading to fruit rot

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply neem cake and compost around roots
  • Spray diluted Bordeaux mixture (1%) every 10 days
  • Prune infected branches post-harvest
  • Ensure canopy is open for air circulation
Insect-Borne

Mango Hopper

Symptoms: Sooty mold on leaves, flower shedding due to hopper infestation

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray neem oil (5%) or garlic-chili extract
  • Introduce ladybird beetles as natural predators
  • Use yellow sticky traps to reduce adult population
  • Remove weeds and clean orchard floor
Physiological

Mango Malformation

Symptoms: Deformed shoots and panicles; affects flowering and yield

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply fermented cow dung + buttermilk mix
  • Remove malformed parts and burn them
  • Spray panchagavya during pre-flowering stage
  • Use Trichoderma viride in soil as bio-control
