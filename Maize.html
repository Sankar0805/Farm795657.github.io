Maize Crop Information

About Maize Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Maize is a vital cereal crop in Andhra Pradesh, grown extensively during both kharif and rabi seasons. Visakhapatnam district has favorable conditions for high-yield maize farming.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil, pH 5.5-7.5

Temperature

18-27°C (64-81°F) ideal for germination and growth

Water Needs

Regular irrigation, especially during flowering and grain filling

Growing Period

90-120 days depending on variety

Common Maize Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Gray Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Gray to tan rectangular lesions on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Crop rotation with non-host crops
  • Use resistant maize varieties
  • Apply neem oil sprays
  • Remove plant debris after harvest
  • Improve airflow by proper spacing
Disease

Turcicum Leaf Blight

Symptoms: Long, gray-green lesions turning brown on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Use Trichoderma-based biofungicides
  • Apply garlic extract spray
  • Encourage early planting to avoid peak infections
  • Maintain field hygiene
  • Use resistant hybrids
Disease

Downy Mildew

Symptoms: White downy growth on underside of leaves, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Hot water seed treatment (55°C for 10 minutes)
  • Neem cake application to soil
  • Spray with cow urine and turmeric mix
  • Ensure good drainage
  • Early sowing to escape infection
