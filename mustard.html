Mustard Crop Information

About Mustard Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Mustard is a significant rabi oilseed crop. It grows well in regions with low rainfall and is often cultivated for its seeds and oil.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soils with pH 6.0–7.5

Temperature

10–25°C for optimal germination and growth

Water Needs

Light irrigation during flowering and seed formation

Growing Period

100–130 days depending on variety

Common Mustard Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

White Rust

Symptoms: White blister-like pustules on leaves and stems

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties
  • Apply neem oil spray weekly
  • Maintain good crop spacing
Disease

Alternaria Blight

Symptoms: Circular brown to black spots on leaves and pods

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply Trichoderma viride-based solution
  • Garlic + turmeric leaf extract spray
  • Remove infected leaves to reduce spread
Disease

Downy Mildew

Symptoms: Yellow patches on upper leaf surface, grayish growth underneath

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray with baking soda + soap solution
  • Use compost tea for foliar spray
  • Avoid overhead watering
