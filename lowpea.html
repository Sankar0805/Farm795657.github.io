Lowpea Crop Information

About Lowpea Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Lowpea (black-eyed pea/lobia) is a legume well-suited to warm climates. It is grown during the kharif and summer seasons in Andhra Pradesh.

Soil Requirements

Light, well-drained sandy or loamy soil, pH 5.5-6.5

Temperature

20–35°C optimal for growth

Water Needs

Drought-tolerant, minimal irrigation needed

Growing Period

60–90 days depending on the variety

Common Lowpea Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Yellow Mosaic Virus (YMV)

Symptoms: Yellow patches on leaves, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant seeds
  • Control whitefly vector using neem oil spray
  • Intercrop with non-host plants
Disease

Powdery Mildew

Symptoms: White powdery coating on leaves and stems

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray baking soda + water + soap solution
  • Use sulfur-based organic sprays
  • Ensure good air circulation in crop rows
Disease

Root Rot

Symptoms: Yellowing, wilting, decaying roots

Organic Remedies:

  • Use Trichoderma in seed treatment
  • Improve soil drainage
  • Rotate crops to break disease cycle
