Chilli Crop Information

About Chilli Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Chilli is a major commercial crop in Andhra Pradesh, with Visakhapatnam district being one of the important chilli-growing regions. The region is famous for its high-quality Guntur chillies.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained sandy loam to clay loam, rich in organic matter, pH 6.5-7.5

Temperature

20-30°C (68-86°F) ideal, sensitive to frost

Water Needs

Moderate irrigation, sensitive to waterlogging

Growing Period

120-150 days from sowing to harvest

Common Chilli Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Anthracnose

Symptoms: Sunken circular spots on fruits, dark lesions on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray neem oil (5ml/liter) every 10 days
  • Apply Trichoderma viride as soil treatment
  • Practice crop rotation with non-host crops
  • Remove and destroy infected plant parts
  • Use resistant varieties like G-4, LCA-235
Bacterial

Bacterial Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Small water-soaked spots turning brown, leaf yellowing

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray copper oxychloride (3g/liter)
  • Use disease-free seeds
  • Practice field sanitation
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
  • Apply Pseudomonas fluorescens as biocontrol
Viral

Chilli Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Upward curling of leaves, stunted growth, reduced fruiting

Organic Remedies:

  • Control whitefly vectors with neem oil
  • Use resistant varieties like Pusa Jwala
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
  • Install yellow sticky traps
  • Practice crop rotation
