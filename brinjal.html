Brinjal Crop Information

About Brinjal Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Brinjal is an important vegetable crop in Andhra Pradesh, widely cultivated in Visakhapatnam due to its favorable climate and soil. Varieties like Pusa Purple and Arka Nidhi are popular in the region.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained fertile loamy soil, pH 6.0-7.0

Water Needs

Moderate and regular watering, avoid water stagnation

100-120 days from transplanting to harvest

Common Brinjal Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Brown or black spots with yellow halo on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray neem oil (5ml/liter) weekly
  • Apply Trichoderma harzianum in soil
  • Use well-drained soil to prevent moisture buildup
  • Remove and destroy infected leaves
  • Use resistant varieties like Arka Sheel
Bacterial

Bacterial Wilt

Symptoms: Sudden wilting of leaves without yellowing, black discoloration in stems

Organic Remedies:

  • Use disease-free seedlings
  • Apply Pseudomonas fluorescens to the soil
  • Use raised beds to improve drainage
  • Rotate crops with non-solanaceous vegetables
  • Ensure field sanitation
Viral

Brinjal Mosaic Virus

Symptoms: Mosaic pattern on leaves, leaf curling, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Control aphid vectors using neem-based sprays
  • Use virus-free seeds and resistant varieties
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
  • Use reflective mulches to deter insects
  • Avoid planting near infected fields
