Sugarcane Crop Information

About Sugarcane Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Sugarcane is widely grown in districts like West Godavari, East Godavari, and Nellore. It is a long-duration crop used for sugar production and biofuels.

Soil Requirements

Loamy, alluvial soil rich in organic matter; pH 6.0–7.5

Temperature

Optimal: 26–32°C; sensitive to frost

Water Needs

High; regular irrigation every 7–10 days

Growing Period

12 to 18 months depending on variety and climate

Common Sugarcane Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Red Rot

Symptoms: Red discoloration of the stalk, foul smell, reduced juice content

Organic Remedies:

  • Use disease-free setts and resistant varieties
  • Apply Trichoderma viride mixed with FYM during planting
  • Remove and burn infected canes
  • Ensure proper drainage to avoid waterlogging
Bacterial

Top Shoot Borer

Symptoms: Drying of central shoot, dead heart appearance in young canes

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply neem oil sprays (3%) early in the season
  • Introduce Trichogramma chilonis as biocontrol
  • Apply cow dung slurry with turmeric near the shoot base
  • Light traps to reduce adult moths
Smut

Smut Disease

Symptoms: Black whip-like structure emerges from top shoot; reduced cane growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Soak seed setts in hot water at 50°C for 30 minutes
  • Remove and destroy smut whips manually
  • Use cow urine + ash spray weekly as preventive
  • Apply neem cake to suppress fungal growth
