Rice Crop Information

About Rice Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Rice is the staple food and primary crop of Andhra Pradesh, with Visakhapatnam district being one of the important rice-growing regions. The coastal climate and fertile soils make it ideal for rice cultivation.

Soil Requirements

Clayey or loamy soil with good water retention, pH 5.0-6.5

Temperature

20-40°C (68-104°F) during growing season

Water Needs

Requires consistent water supply, typically flooded fields

Growing Period

3-6 months depending on variety

Common Rice Viruses and Organic Remedies

Virus

Rice Tungro Virus

Symptoms: Yellow-orange discoloration of leaves, stunted growth, reduced tillering

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply fermented plant juice (FPJ) from fast-growing plants
  • Use neem cake in standing water to deter vectors
  • Introduce duck farming in rice fields to control insects
  • Practice synchronous planting in the area
  • Spray garlic-chili extract weekly during outbreaks
Virus

Rice Grassy Stunt Virus

Symptoms: Excessive tillering, narrow pale green leaves, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply compost tea to boost plant immunity
  • Use reflective mulches to deter brown planthoppers
  • Introduce predatory spiders to control vectors
  • Rotate with non-host crops like mung beans
  • Spray neem oil solution every 10 days
Virus

Rice Ragged Stunt Virus

Symptoms: Ragged leaf edges, dark green leaves, stunted growth

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply silica-rich amendments to strengthen cell walls
  • Use botanical insecticides from derris or tobacco
  • Introduce mirid bugs as natural predators
  • Practice early planting to avoid peak vector populations
  • Spray soap solution (1 tbsp per liter) weekly
