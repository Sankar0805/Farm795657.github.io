Sunflower Crop Information

About Sunflower Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Sunflower is a high-yield oilseed crop suited for varied agro-climatic conditions. It is grown widely due to its drought resistance and high oil content.

Soil Requirements

Loamy to sandy loam soils with good drainage and pH 6.5–8.0

Temperature

20–25°C is ideal for growth and flowering

Water Needs

Moderate irrigation, especially during flowering and seed filling

Growing Period

90–120 days depending on the variety

Common Sunflower Diseases and Organic Remedies

Disease

Downy Mildew

Symptoms: Pale yellow patches on upper leaves, white mold underneath

Organic Remedies:

  • Use disease-free seeds
  • Apply compost tea foliar spray
  • Ensure proper drainage and avoid overhead watering
Disease

Alternaria Leaf Spot

Symptoms: Brown or black circular spots on leaves

Organic Remedies:

  • Neem oil spray every 7 days
  • Remove infected leaves early
  • Crop rotation to reduce recurrence
Disease

Rust

Symptoms: Reddish-brown pustules on lower leaf surface

Organic Remedies:

  • Spray garlic oil solution
  • Improve air circulation by proper spacing
  • Use resistant varieties if available
