Tomato Crop Information

About Tomato Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Tomato is a major cash crop in Andhra Pradesh, with Visakhapatnam district contributing significantly to production. The region's climate supports year-round cultivation with proper irrigation management.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained sandy loam, rich in organic matter, pH 6.0-7.0

Temperature

20-27°C (68-80°F) ideal, sensitive to frost

Water Needs

Regular irrigation, 2-3 inches per week

Growing Period

90-120 days from transplanting

Common Tomato Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Early Blight

Symptoms: Dark spots with concentric rings on leaves, yellowing, defoliation

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply copper-based fungicides every 7-10 days
  • Use baking soda spray (1 tbsp/gallon water)
  • Practice crop rotation (3-4 year cycle)
  • Remove infected plant debris
  • Improve air circulation between plants
Bacterial

Bacterial Wilt

Symptoms: Sudden wilting, stem discoloration, white ooze in water test

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties (Arka Abha, Arka Alok)
  • Solarize soil before planting
  • Apply biocontrol agents (Pseudomonas)
  • Avoid waterlogged conditions
  • Practice strict sanitation
Viral

Tomato Leaf Curl Virus

Symptoms: Upward leaf curling, stunting, reduced fruiting

Organic Remedies:

  • Use resistant varieties (Arka Rakshak)
  • Control whiteflies with neem oil
  • Install yellow sticky traps
  • Remove infected plants immediately
  • Use reflective mulches
