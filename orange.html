Orange Crop Information

About Orange Cultivation in Andhra Pradesh

Orange cultivation is prominent in the Rayalaseema and coastal belts. Nagpur and Sathgudi varieties are preferred for juice and fresh markets.

Soil Requirements

Well-drained loamy soil with pH 5.5–7.5

Temperature

Ideal range: 15–35°C; avoid frost and extreme heat

Water Needs

Weekly irrigation; avoid both drought and waterlogging

Growing Period

Fruit bearing starts in 3 years; peak yield by 5–8 years

Common Orange Diseases and Organic Remedies

Fungal

Citrus Canker

Symptoms: Raised brown spots on leaves and fruit, premature fruit drop

Organic Remedies:

  • Prune infected branches and destroy them
  • Spray diluted copper oxychloride every 15 days
  • Use neem leaf extract as foliar spray
  • Ensure proper plant spacing and air flow
Viral

Citrus Tristeza Virus (CTV)

Symptoms: Yellowing leaves, twig dieback, small fruit size

Organic Remedies:

  • Plant virus-free certified saplings
  • Control aphids with neem oil (2.5%) spray
  • Intercrop with marigold to repel vectors
  • Remove infected plants immediately
Physiological

Zinc Deficiency

Symptoms: Interveinal chlorosis (yellowing), poor fruit development

Organic Remedies:

  • Apply zinc-enriched compost or rock dust
  • Use foliar spray with diluted seaweed extract
  • Add cow dung slurry and vermicompost
  • Mulch regularly to retain soil nutrients
